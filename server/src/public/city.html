The problem today

Seattle hosts hundreds of permitted events every year. Right now, every one of them is an island.

📱

Fragmented apps

Each festival builds its own app, or skips it entirely. Residents typically download, use once, then delete. No continuity, no shared data.

📣

No emergency channel

When weather cancels a festival or a street closes unexpectedly, there's no fast, direct way to reach the people who are already on their way.

🔍

No visibility

The city issues permits but has no aggregate view of attendance patterns, neighborhood distribution, or accessibility across events.

Built for city government

Canopy isn't just a consumer app. It's infrastructure designed around the needs of the departments that make events happen.

Permitting

Permit ID badges

Every event card displays its city permit ID. Residents can see at a glance that an event is legitimate and city-sanctioned.

Public Safety

Emergency push notifications

Cancel an event, reroute foot traffic, or issue a weather alert — pushed directly to the phones of people who saved that event.

Equity

Accessibility filters

Users can filter by wheelchair access, ASL interpreters, sensory-friendly times, and free admission — making equity visible, not buried.

Transit

Real-time transit integration

Every event shows nearby bus stops with live arrival times via OneBusAway, plus street closure overlays from city data feeds.

Data

City analytics dashboard

Aggregate, anonymized data on event engagement by neighborhood, category, and time — exportable as CSV or JSON for city reporting.

Communications

Official city badge

City-sponsored events carry a "City of Seattle Official" badge with the civic seal, distinguishing them from private events.

Value for every department

Different offices, different priorities — Canopy addresses them all with one platform.

Office of Arts & Culture

  • Neighborhood equity heatmap

  • Accessibility-first design

SDOT / Transit

  • Bike rack and parking info

Mayor's Office

  • City of Seattle Official badge
  • Civic alerts channel (opt-in)
  • Public-facing engagement stats
  • Unified event calendar for communications

Built to last

Canopy is production software, not a prototype.

Native iOS

Built in SwiftUI with offline-first architecture. Works without cell service at crowded outdoor events.

Privacy-first

No account required. No email collected. No tracking. Location is used on-device only for transit directions. CCPA compliant.

Open API

RESTful API with JSON and CSV export. The city can query its own events programmatically for internal dashboards and reporting.
